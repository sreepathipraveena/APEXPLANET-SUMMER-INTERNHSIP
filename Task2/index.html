Frequently Asked Questions

A general rule of thumb is to consume at least 2 to 3 liters (about 8-12 glasses) of water daily. However, requirements vary based on physical activity, climate, and body size. AeroFit lets you adjust your custom targets in the Profile settings.
Body Mass Index (BMI) is calculated as Weight in kilograms divided by Height in meters squared (\(\text{BMI} = \text{weight} / \text{height}^2\)). While BMI is a quick screening tool for weight categories, it does not directly measure body fat percentage or muscle mass, which means athletes might obtain an 'overweight' ranking despite being highly fit.
AeroFit utilizes the scientifically recognized Mifflin-St Jeor Equation to calculate your Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR), which is then multiplied by your physical activity factor to determine your Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE). This gives you the calories needed to maintain your current weight.
Cardiovascular exercise (like running, cycling) focuses on increasing your heart rate and improving respiratory endurance, burning higher calories during the activity. Strength training (like lifting weights) targets muscle growth and bone density, increasing your metabolic rate so you burn more calories at rest.

Daily Health Tip

Nutrition

Hydrate Before You Feel Thirsty

By the time your body signals thirst, you are already mildly dehydrated. Keep a water bottle handy and take small sips regularly throughout the day to sustain energy and muscle performance.
